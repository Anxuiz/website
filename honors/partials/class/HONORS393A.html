Rhetoric of Science

Rhetoric of Science was a fun class that I didn't expect to be so interested in. We got to discuss many ways in which rhetoric affects how science is communicated to non-scientists, and identify methods to improve communication to achieve better understanding.

I also really enjoyed how the class was structured. The entire quarter revolved around producing a final paper which would be considered research quality. Assignments given before the final paper were to prepare material and get us thinking early about how we wanted to analyze the text we had chosen. For my final paper, I chose to analyze Yuri Lazebnik's article "Can I biologist fix a radio? -- Or, what I learned while studying apoptosis".
