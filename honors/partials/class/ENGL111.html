Composition: Literature

ENGL 111 is a basic requirement for composition at UW, so I didn't originally put much thought into it. I know I wanted to do something with literature because I enjoy writing about books and stories more than random topics. One thing that makes ENGL 111 unique is that each section is taught by a different grad student in English, but you don't know who will be your instructor or what the class will be like until basically the first day. I remember telling myself, "As long as I don't get someone who teaches 16th century poetry, I'll be alright."

Well, that's what I got. Imagine my surprise when I walk in day one and find out we'll be spending the entire quarter reading Paradise Lost, a 17th century English epic that makes Shakespeare seem like light reading. The class had to be the most hardcore ENGL 111 section that quarter, and though I remember it being difficult I was glad to have an instructor who was extremely interested in the topic every class and wanted to share his enthusiam for it with everyone in the class.

This course made my high school literature class seem like a cakewalk, but in my struggles with the material and connecting ideas I wrote one of my favorite papers here at UW. In this paper I draw an analogy between Satan in Paradise Lost and Lou from the movie Nightcrawler. It was fun to compare a weighty character from a very classic piece of literature to a movie I had just seen a few months before.
