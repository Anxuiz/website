UWashington Formula Motorsports

During my internship of the summer of 2015, I was sitting in my apartment at the end of a long day at work and realized that maybe it would be good to have a hobby that did not involve staring at a screen. I remembered there was a club that builds race cars, and that seemed like a good way to get off the computer so I applied. After a brief interview, I was accepted onto the electronics technical team.

Joining the electronics team, I had no knowledge of electronics or really any physics from the electricity and magnetism section of my AP Physics class. But it was fun to learn quickly an entire new subject from the ground up just by studying and learning as a went. During the 2015-2016 school year I also ended up taking the honors physics series, so that was benefitial in giving me a bit better foundational knowledge of the circuits I was designing.

T27 (2015-2016)

This year I was on the electronics technical team and worked on the Brake System Plausibility Device, a circuit board on the car part of the safety system that ensures that if the brake and throttle are both pressed simultaneously for too long the car will shut off.
