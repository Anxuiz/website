Formal Verification

Currently I am working with the Programming Languages and Software Engineering (PLSE) Group to learn the basics of formal verification. Formal verification is the practice of modeling computer programs using rigorous mathematics to prove properties about these programs. Because there is really no undergraduate classes that directly address this field I am currently independently studying Software Foundations, a textbook that teaches the basics of formally verifying programs using the Coq proof assistant. While studying this book I am meeting with a graduate student who is helping me understand the field and answer questions I have about the book.

Publications

The research project I worked with has made several publications since I started.

Doug Woos, James R. Wilcox, Steve Anton, Zachary Tatlock, Michael D. Ernst, and Thomas Anderson. Planning for Change in a Formal Verification of the Raft Consensus Protocol. CPP 2016, St. Petersburg, FL, January 2016. Pdf Copy.

This paper covers the experience of proofing the Raft consensus protocol in Coq using the Verdi framework. This was what I spent most of my time during my research working on.

James R. Wilcox, Doug Woos, Pavel Panchekha, Zachary Tatlock, Xi Wang, Michael D. Ernst, and Thomas Anderson. Verdi: A Framework for Formally Verifying Distributed System Implementations. In PLDI 2015. Pdf Copy

This paper was the first paper for the Verdi project and outlines the theoretical framework in which distributed systems could be proven.
